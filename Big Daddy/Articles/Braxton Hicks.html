BRAXTON HICKS

“Darling, I think I’m having a contraction!”

“OHMYGOD is the bag packed? Did we buy nappies? Do I have time to have a sandwich?”

“Oh no, wait. Oh. I think it’s just a Bloody Hicks.”

Braxton Hicks (or Bloody Hicks, as we like to call them) contractions are the fakers of the pregnancy world. They’re not real contractions, but are the body’s way of practising for the real thing. They don’t even mean that labour is imminent. During a Braxton Hicks contraction, a woman’s bump will tense up as the womb contracts, and then relax again.

Braxton Hicks are really, really common. They usually happen during the third trimester, although some women do get them earlier. (mother) may have a couple every day or every hour, or she may not have any at all. Braxton Hicks can be triggered by sex, being active, someone touching the bump or dehydration.

If (mother) is finding the Braxton Hicks contractions uncomfortable, changing positions can help them to ease off. Alternatively, (mother) could try sipping water or having a relaxing bath.

Sometimes, it can be difficult to tell the difference between those cheeky fakers and the real thing.

Braxton Hicks:

  • Not painful, but can be uncomfortable.
  • Irregular.
  • Stop after a fairly short time, or a change in position.

 

Labour contractions:

  • Painful. 
  • Regular, getting longer in duration and closer together.
  • Don’t stop!
  • Are often accompanied by other labour signs.

If you’re not sure if the contractions are real - or if there’s something weird and it don’t look good - who you gonna call? The midwives! Obvs.
