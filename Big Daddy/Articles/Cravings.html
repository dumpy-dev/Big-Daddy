CRAVINGS

Picture the scene (it may already be familiar). It’s midnight and you’ve just crawled between the covers after an evening of Netflix, Sky Sports or Antiques Roadshow (whatever floats your boat). Sleep cannot come soon enough.

(mother): Do you know what I could really eat?

You: [Oh god. Please let it be something we have in the house.]

(mother): Some really cold melon. 

You: [Thank god] No problem! We have a very cold melon in the fridge. I will go and chop it up for you. Anything for a huge but lovely mum-to-be.

(mother): But that’s a cantaloupe melon in the fridge!

You: Is that not a melon?

(mother): I just really fancy a honeydew melon.

You: [Kill me now] I’ll be right back.

(mother): While you’re out, can you get me some really fresh banana ice cream?

Cravings in pregnancy are almost inevitable. Apparently, it has something to do with hormones. In short, (mother) can demand any food she likes until the baby appears and there’s not a lot you can do about it. 

For your amusement, here are some of the most common pregnancy cravings.

  • chocolate
  • sweets (particularly sour ones)
  • ice cream
  • fruit
  • pickles
  • cheese
  • meat (can cause all sorts of problems if she’s a veggie)
  • spicy food

… and some of the weirdest pregnancy cravings we’ve heard of:

  • ice
  • heartburn tablets
  • mustard
  • toothpaste
  • dry teabags*
  • soap*
  • mud*
  • chalk*
  • coal*
  • sand*
  • burnt matches*

*If (mother) wants to eat any of these things (or anything else that is, frankly, inedible or toxic), don’t let her do it. She may have an uncommon condition called pica (which comes from the Latin word for magpie - those greedy birds - pica pica), where mums-to-be crave non-food items. It’s important that she talks to her midwife about it for advice and support.

And in case you’re wondering, pica has nothing to do with Pikachu (whose name apparently comes from pika, the sound an electric spark makes in Japan, and chu, the sound a Japanese mouse makes).
