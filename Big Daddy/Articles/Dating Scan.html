DATING SCAN

Despite its name, the dating scan unfortunately doesn’t involve a candlelit dinner for two, followed by Netflix and chill… It’s actually an ultrasound scan which (mother) will have when she’s around 12 weeks pregnant.

At the scan, the sonographer will:

  • tell you exactly how far along (mother) is and give you a more accurate due date. This could be either earlier or later than the due date you were expecting.
  • check how many babies are in there.
  • check the baby is growing normally and that there aren’t any problems with their development.
  • not confirm the gender (it’s too early to tell).

 

(mother) will be allowed to take someone with her to the scan, so you should go with her if you possibly can. In the UK, dads-to-be are entitled to unpaid time off work to accompany the mum-to-be to up to two antenatal appointments. If you can, you really should go. It’s a pretty big moment, seeing your baby on the big screen. “Aha,” you can say. “Look what I’ve created. I have made BABY.” Nothing beats it. 

Before the scan, (mother) will have been offered screening for Down’s syndrome. If she agrees to this, she’ll have had a blood test before the scan. During the scan, the sonographer will measure the fluid at the back of the baby’s neck and will combine the results of both to tell you the likelihood of the baby having Down’s syndrome.

Most hospitals will give you the option of being able to purchase photos from the scan, so don’t forget to take some cash along in case they don’t accept cards.

Some mums and dads-to-be can feel a bit anxious about the dating scan, as it’s the first time they’ll be able to find out if their baby is healthy. Try not to dwell too much on these kind of worries - most scans show a perfectly normal baby (which admittedly will look a little like an alien).  Seeing your baby for the first time can suddenly make everything seem much more real, and some parents-to-be like to mark the occasion by going out for lunch or getting a takeaway and gazing at the photo of your little alien. 

“When we went for the dating scan at 12 weeks, I realised that nobody had ever checked that my partner was actually pregnant! All we had to go on was a home pregnancy test, which had been done weeks ago. What if there had been a mistake and there was nothing in there? But when we saw our little wriggler doing somersaults on the screen, it suddenly sunk in - we were having a baby!” - Chris, London
