DRIVING

Can women continue to drive during pregnancy? In short, yes - although there may come a time when (mother)'s bump is so big that either her arms won’t reach the steering wheel or her feet won’t reach the pedals. This might make driving a little more difficult. If this happens, be nice and offer her lifts if you can.

If (mother) is driving during pregnancy, she should make sure that she:

  • wears her seatbelt with the lower strap sitting under her bump and the top strap going over her collarbone. She should avoid lap-only seatbelts as these could be dangerous for the baby if the car brakes suddenly, although any seatbelt is better than none.
  • avoids driving for long distances if possible, and takes a break every 90 minutes or so to stretch her legs and keep the blood circulating. 
  • stays hydrated in the car and keeps snacks handy.
  • gets checked out by a doctor if she has any kind of accident, even if it’s really minor and she feels completely fine.

And contrary to rumour, airbags are completely safe (provided a seatbelt is being used) and will help to protect the woman and her baby.
