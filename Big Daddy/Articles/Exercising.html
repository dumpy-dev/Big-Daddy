EXERCISING

While (mother) is spending most of the day with her head over the toilet, exercise will probably be  the last thing on her mind. In fact, don’t even mention the E word at this stage unless you fancy sleeping on the sofa (or worse).

Once (mother) is feeling better - usually around the second trimester - a little light exercise can be really beneficial for her and the baby. In fact, there’s evidence that staying fit can even help avoid problems during later pregnancy and labour. A lot of women find swimming, walking and yoga are all great ways to keep fit during pregnancy.

Before (mother) deadlifts, have a read of these handy tips to keep her safe while exercising:

(mother) should:

  • keep up her normal level of exercise as long as she feels comfortable
  • keep hydrated
  • warm up and cool down before and after exercising
  • tell her fitness instructor or trainer that she is pregnant

(mother) shouldn’t:

  • exhaust herself (she should be able to hold a conversation whilst exercising - otherwise she is probably overdoing it)
  • suddenly take up a new and strenuous exercise or sport
  • overheat during exercise
  • participate in activities with a risk of falling, such as ice hockey or contact sports
  • lie flat on her back for long periods after 16 weeks of pregnancy
