LABOUR

The days of anxiously pacing the hospital corridors and trying to ignore the sounds of the labour ward are gone. Nowadays, dads-to-be are usually expected to be at the mother’s side throughout labour. So you chaps should probably know what to expect. But we’ll keep this brief, as you don’t need all the gory details. 

Like the musketeers, Lions and components of a BLT, there are three stages of labour:

1. The first stage

Contractions begin and the mum’s cervix will start to open (dilate). You (or (mother)) will need to call the midwife and get their advice on what to do next. Fasten your seatbelts - it’s going to be a bumpy ride. During this stage, you’ll travel to wherever you plan to have the baby and things will really start to heat up. This tends to be the longest stage of labour, but you ain’t heard nothing yet.

2. The second stage

Once (mother)’s cervix is 10cm dilated (and the pain is at eleven), the second stage begins. There might be a short breather, but just when you think you’re out, they pull you back in. *SPOILER ALERT* It’s time for her to push and deliver the baby.

3. The third stage

Once the baby is born, the third stage begins. (mother)’s contractions will start up again and she will deliver the placenta. She can opt to have an injection to speed this up.

The fourth stage is obviously the tea and toast, and cuddles with your baby, but apparently that’s not technically an official term. Yet.
