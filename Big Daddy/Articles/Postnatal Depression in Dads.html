POSTNATAL DEPRESSION IN DADS

Did you know that postnatal depression can also affect dads, as well as mums? Studies have shown that more than 1 in 3 new fathers have concerns about their own mental health, and 1 in 10 suffer from postnatal depression.

Becoming a dad (whether it’s for the first time or the fifth) can be a stressful and emotional time. Even though you may be completely in love with your tiny (but very stinky and screamy) bundle, there’s no denying that your lifestyle and relationship will change overnight. What with sleep deprivation, possible financial worries, and feelings of sadness or guilt at returning to work, it’s not really surprising that having a baby can have a significant impact on dads’ mental health. 

The symptoms of postnatal depression are varied, and can include:

  • Feeling very low
  • Feeling like you don’t want to do anything, or avoiding friends
  • Feeling like you can’t cope
  • Feeling guilty about not being a ‘good enough’ dad or not loving your baby enough
  • Wanting to cry
  • Problems sleeping or vivid nightmares
  • Panic attacks
  • Comfort eating or loss of appetite
  • Problems concentrating
  • Overwhelming anxiety about things that wouldn’t normally bother you
  • Obsessing about your baby’s health or safety
  • Disturbing thoughts about harming yourself or your baby
  • Thinking about death
 

It’s so important to remember that low mood and anxiety is incredibly common in new parents, and doesn’t mean that you have postnatal depression. If you’re feeling a bit low, try and get out and take some exercise or meet up with a friend or family member and do something you enjoy (ideally with the baby!). If you know any other dads, perhaps suggest going for a walk with the buggies and try and open up to them about how you’re feeling. It might seem like the hardest thing in the world, but chances are they’ve felt the same way.

If you’re worried that your low mood isn’t going away, or if you just don’t feel ‘right’ in yourself, you should get in touch with your GP right away as there might be other options which could help. If you ever feel like you’d like to talk to someone - even if it’s in the middle of the night - you can always call The Samaritans’ 24 hour helpline on 116 123. 

Whether you’re just feeling a bit low or have postnatal depression, the two most important things to remember are that - firstly - you’re not alone and - secondly - just the sight of your ugly face will make your baby feel happy. And if that’s not being a great dad, we don’t know what is.
