TOP TIPS TO GET READY FOR B-DAY

Preparation is key. Here’s our handy list of things to do before B-Day.

  • Make sure the hospital bag is packed and ready to go from around 36 weeks. If there are things that you’ll need to chuck in at the last minute (such as toothbrushes or phone chargers), keep a handy list in the bag so you can check those things off at the last minute. Have a look at our hospital bag checklist!
  • Try and pack the hospital bag yourself (under (mother)’s close supervision). Trust us, it helps if you know the difference between a maternity pad and a breast pad in the heat of the moment.
  • Construct the cot, crib or moses basket - whatever the baby is going to be sleeping in. Work out how long you think it’s going to take you, then double it. 
  • If you can, get batch cooking and freeze some meals in advance. If you’re more Gordon Brown than Gordon Ramsay, find some tasty and healthy meals in the supermarket to keep in the freezer. You’ll thank us when you’ve changed your 23rd nappy of the day and feel ready to drop.
  • Do an online shop, but keep all the items in your basket and ready to check out. Make sure you’ve chosen plenty of tea bags, coffee and snacks. Then, when B-day comes around, you can schedule the delivery for when you know you’ll be home.
  • Make sure you have plenty of nappies!
  • Search YouTube for tutorials on how to give really good shoulder, back, hand and foot massages. Ask (mother) if she’d like a massage, and practise your new skills.
  • If (mother) has any birth preferences, try and talk them through with her. Does she want to have an epidural, or would she find a water bath helpful? Make sure you know how she feels so that you can help speak for her during labour.
  • Put important numbers - the hospital or midwife unit, and her family’s contact details - onto your phone in advance. Perhaps also write them on a piece of paper and pack in the hospital bag, just in case.
  • If you’re planning on driving to a hospital or maternity unit to have the baby, do a recce to make sure you know where you’re going and where to park. If the parking machine only takes change, make sure you pack some cash in the hospital bag.
  • If you have a car, make sure the car seat is fitted and you know how to get it in and out easily. And while you’re there, check there’s plenty of fuel in the tank.
